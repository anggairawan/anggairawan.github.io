Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of control theory, embedded computing, and deployable machine intelligence — asking how algorithmic decision-making can be made to run reliably on the hardware that actually exists in the physical world.

I

Deployable Intelligence on Resource-Constrained Hardware

From my MSc thesis on representing symbolic controllers with deep neural networks to recent embedded ML work with Edge Impulse, I am drawn to the fundamental question of how learned or rule-based decision models can be compressed, approximated, and executed under strict memory and compute budgets — without sacrificing safety or correctness guarantees.

II

Real-Time Control & Hybrid Systems

My work spans fixed-point power electronics control, attitude estimation for UAVs and rockets, and deterministic firmware scheduling for thousands of field-deployed IoT nodes. I am interested in how hybrid and symbolic control frameworks can bridge formal guarantees with practical real-time implementation on microcontroller-class platforms.

III

Distributed & Autonomous Embedded Systems

Operating 5,000+ IoT devices under intermittent connectivity taught me that robustness and scalability in autonomous systems demand more than a good algorithm — they require hardware–software co-design, offline-capable state machines, and rigorous validation pipelines. I am interested in extending these principles toward autonomous, distributed robotic systems.

IV

Hardware–Software Co-Design for Intelligent Systems

From PCB layout to RTOS scheduling to ML model quantization, I approach intelligent systems as vertically integrated problems. My interest lies in the co-engineering of electronics, firmware, and learning algorithms — treating the physical platform as a first-class design constraint rather than an afterthought.
